Phishing Attack

The most common form of cybercrime is phishing, where the attacker tries to obtain critical information by impersonating a trusted entity. The initial step in the 
phishing attack is usually an email, SMS, or social media notification that appears legitimate. The email or notification may be from a bank, university, payment 
gateway, or popular website. The notification often claims that the user’s account will be blocked, the payment has been declined, or immediate verification is 
necessary. These notifications often include a link that takes the victim to a fake website, which looks almost identical to the original website.

When the victim enters the login information, personal details, or banking information on the fake site created by the hacker, the hacker collects the 
information. In some cases, the phishing messages also contain attachments that install viruses on the computer. Students are also targeted by 
phishing emails that contain fake internship opportunities, scholarships, or password reset emails.

Once the attackers gain access, they may misuse the accounts, steal money, or even carry out identity theft. Victims may lose access to emails or social media 
accounts, and their personal information may also be revealed. Phishing attacks are effective because of the urgency and trust associated with them. It is 
essential to check the links and messages to avoid such attacks.
